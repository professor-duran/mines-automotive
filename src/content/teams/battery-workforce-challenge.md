## About

The Battery Workforce Challenge is a U.S. Department of Energy and Stellantis-sponsored competition that pairs universities with industry partners to redesign production-vehicle battery packs. Our team is a cross-institutional partnership between Colorado School of Mines and Arapahoe Community College. Mines contributes engineering students and engineering faculty leadership (Prof. Adam Duran and Prof. Polina Brodsky as co-PIs); ACC contributes automotive technology students and shares its facilities and expertise in vehicle servicing and maintenance, under the faculty leadership of Prof. Jeff Tipsword.

Our team placed 1st nationally in Year 1 (2023 to 2024) and 4th in Year 2 (2024 to 2025), with approximately $255,000 in total program value across the two years (DOE/Stellantis base grant, Chevron sponsorship, student travel stipends, competition prizes).

## What students do

Team members work on cell selection, thermal management, battery management systems, pack mechanical design, safety analysis, fabrication, vehicle servicing, and final integration into the sponsor vehicle. The program is cross-functional by design, pairing Mines engineering students with ACC automotive technology students and making them full partners with real decision authority on servicing, maintenance, and build questions.

## How to join

BWC recruits annually at both Mines and ACC. Prior coursework in battery systems, power electronics, or vehicle service helps but is not required. Teams are kept deliberately small and accountable; students should expect weekly meetings, biweekly deliverables, and competition travel.
